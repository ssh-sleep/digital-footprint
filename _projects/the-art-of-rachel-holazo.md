## Overview

Rachel Holazo was a painter and visual artist based in Davao City. Since 2007 I have
managed her estate — handling the business, legal, and curatorial side of her body of work
so that the art itself is preserved, accessible, and properly represented.

This is not a typical portfolio project. It sits at the intersection of intellectual
property law, archival practice, and cultural stewardship — and it has no finish line.

## What I Do

- Appraisal, storage, and preservation of original artworks
- Cataloging the full body of work with provenance documentation
- Licensing and copyright management for reproductions and commercial use
- Organizing solo exhibitions and retrospectives
- Coordinating loans to museums and galleries for displays and group shows
- Overseeing all legal matters related to the estate

## Why It Matters

Most artists' estates dissolve or go undocumented. The administrative work behind
keeping a body of work alive — legally protected, physically preserved, and publicly
visible — is invisible until it isn't done. I've been doing it for nearly two decades.
